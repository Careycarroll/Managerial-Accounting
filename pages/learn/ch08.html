Overhead Cost Variances
and Management Control

Chapter 7 covered direct-cost variances. Now we extend variance analysis to indirect costs -- variable and fixed overhead -- completing the full 4-variance picture.

Learning Objectives

  1. Explain the similarities and differences in planning variable and fixed overhead costs
  2. Develop budgeted variable overhead cost rates and budgeted fixed overhead cost rates
  3. Compute the variable overhead flexible-budget, efficiency, and spending variances
  4. Compute the fixed overhead flexible-budget, spending, and production-volume variances
  5. Show how the 4-variance analysis approach reconciles actual overhead with allocated overhead
  6. Explain the relationship between the sales-volume variance and the production-volume variance
  7. Calculate variances in activity-based costing
  8. Examine the use of overhead variances in nonmanufacturing settings
